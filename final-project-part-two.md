# Wireframes / storyboards
For Part II, I developed a scroll-based storyboard and began building high-fidelity charts in Tableau. These visuals follow my Part I outline and represent the critical points in the story arc. Each section corresponds to a section of the final Shorthand story and will be refined further in Part III based on user feedback.

**Storyboard Sections:**
1. **Intro** ‚Äì ‚ÄúWill AI take your job or transform it?‚Äù Hero image + one-liner hook.
2. **Adoption Trends** ‚Äì Bar chart showing AI adoption by industry.
3. **Layoff Impact** ‚Äì Timeline showing major layoffs attributed to AI (2020‚Äì2023).
4. **Job Creation** ‚Äì Side-by-side bar showing AI-related job creation.
5. **Exposure Risk** ‚Äì Scatterplot: AI exposure vs average wage (AIOE dataset).
6. **Worker Sentiment** ‚Äì Stacked bar of attitudes (hopeful, neutral, fearful).
7. **Conclusion** ‚Äì A summary and call to action with resources for upskilling.

# User research

## Target audience
The story is designed for early-career professionals and graduate students at CMU studying technology, design, entertainment, and policy ‚Äî fields increasingly impacted by AI-driven job changes.

## Interview script
The primary goals of my interviews were:
- Assess whether the visuals and narrative are clear and meaningful.
- Identify which elements are most helpful or confusing.
- Gather suggestions for improvement.

| Goal                         | Questions to Ask                                               |
|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Understand user engagement    | What part of the story felt most relevant or memorable to you? |
| Test clarity of visuals       | Were any visualizations difficult to understand or interpret? |
| Assess emotional resonance    | Did anything surprise you or change how you think about AI?    |
| Identify improvement          | What could make the story more engaging and helpful for you?   |

---

## Interview findings

| Questions                     | Interview 1                  | Interview 2                  | Interview 3                  |
|--------------------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Most relevant section?        | Exposure risk scatterplot     | Layoff timeline               | New job creation bar chart    |
| Surprise or insight?          | High-paying jobs high risk    | Surprised non-coders hit too  | Optimistic after job gains seen |
| Suggestions for improvement   | Color-coded jobs              | Include a call to action      | Better labels and callouts    |

---

## Identified changes for Part III

| Research Synthesis                       | Changes for Part III                                               |
|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Timeline visualization lacked clarity    | Simplified to 5‚Äì6 key events, color-coded by industry              |
| Scatterplot labeling was weak             | Added labels and tooltips with occupations                         |
| Lack of interactivity                     | Attempted optional filtering where feasible                       |
| Missing conclusion or takeaway            | Added actionable next steps at the end                             |
